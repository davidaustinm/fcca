Exercise 22.

Suppose \(S\subset\C\) is closed and bounded and all points of \(S\) are isolated points of \(S\text{.}\) Show that \(S\) is finite, as follows:
  1. For each \(z\in S\) we can choose \(\phi(z)>0\) so that \(D[z,\phi(z)]\) contains no points of \(S\) except \(z\text{.}\) Show that \(\phi\) is continuous. (Hint: This is really easy if you use the first definition of continuity in Section 2.1.)
  2. Assume \(S\) is non-empty. By the Extreme Value Theorem A.0.1, \(\phi\) has a minimum value, \(r_0>0\text{.}\) Let \(r=r_0/2\text{.}\) Since \(S\) is bounded, it lies in a disk \(D[0,M]\) for some \(M\text{.}\) Show that the small disks \(D[z,r]\text{,}\) for \(z\in S\text{,}\) are disjoint and lie in \(D[0,M+r]\text{.}\)
  3. Find a bound on the number of such small disks. (Hint: Compare the areas of \(D[z,r]\) and \(D[0,M+r]\text{.}\))
