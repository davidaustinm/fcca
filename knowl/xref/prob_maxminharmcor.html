Exercise 13.

Prove Corollary 8.2.7: Assume that \(u\) is harmonic in a region \(G\) and has a weak local maximum at \(a\in G\text{.}\)
  1. If \(G\) is simply connected then apply Theorem 8.2.4 to \(\exp(u(z)+iv(z)))\text{,}\) where \(v\) is a harmonic conjugate of \(u\text{.}\) Conclude that \(u\) is constant on \(G\text{.}\)
  2. If \(G\) is not simply connected, then the above argument applies to \(u\) on any disk \(D[a,R] \subset G\text{.}\) Conclude that the partials \(u_x\) and \(u_y\) are zero on \(G\text{,}\) and adapt the argument of Theorem 2.4.2 to show that \(u\) is constant.
