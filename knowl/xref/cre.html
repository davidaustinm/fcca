Theorem 2.3.1.

  1. Suppose \(f\) is differentiable at \(z_0 = x_0 + i y_0\text{.}\) Then the partial derivatives of \(f\) exist and satisfy
    \begin{equation} \fderiv fx(z_0) \= -i \, \fderiv fy(z_0) \,\tag{2.2} \end{equation}
    and the derivative is given by
    \begin{equation*} f'(z_0) \= \fderiv fx(z_0) \,\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. Suppose \(f\) is a complex function such that the partial derivatives \(\fderiv fx\) and \(\fderiv fy\) exist in an open disk centered at \(z_0\) and are continuous at \(z_0\text{.}\) If these partial derivatives satisfy {(2.2)} then \(f\) is differentiable atÂ \(z_0\text{.}\)
