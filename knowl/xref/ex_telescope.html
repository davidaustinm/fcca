Example 7.2.3.

Another series whose limit we can compute by manipulating the partial sums is
\begin{align*} \sum_{k\ge1} \frac1{k^2 + k} \amp \= \lim_{n\to\infty}\sum_{k=1}^n \left(\frac1k-\frac1{k+1}\right)\\ \amp \= \lim_{n\to\infty}\left(1-\frac12+\frac12 -\frac13 +\frac13 -\frac14 + \dots + \frac1n-\frac1{n+1}\right)\\ \amp \= \lim_{n\to\infty}\left(1-\frac1{n+1}\right) \= 1 \, \text{.} \end{align*}
A series where most of the terms cancel like this is called telescoping.
