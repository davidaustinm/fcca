Theorem 9.1.9. Casorati
 1 
Felice Casorati (1835–1890).
–Weierstraß.

If \(z_0\) is an essential singularity of \(f\) and \(r\) is any positive real number, then every \(w \in \C\) is arbitrarily close to a point in \(f(\Dp[z_0,r])\text{.}\) That is, for any \(w \in \C\) and any \(\epsilon > 0\) there exists \(z \in \Dp[z_0,r]\) such that \(|w-f(z)| \lt \epsilon\text{.}\)
