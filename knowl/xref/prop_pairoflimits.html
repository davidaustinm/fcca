Proposition 7.1.10.

  1. Exponentials beat polynomials: for any polynomial \(p(n)\) (with complex coefficients) and any \(c \in \C\) with \(\abs{c} > 1\text{,}\)
    \begin{equation*} \lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{p(n)}{c^n}\=0 \,\text{.} \end{equation*}
  2. Factorials beat exponentials: for any \(c \in \C\text{,}\)
    \begin{equation*} \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{c^n}{n!}\=0 \,\text{.} \end{equation*}
