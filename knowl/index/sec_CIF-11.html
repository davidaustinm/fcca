First, we need a notion of the inside of a simple closed path. The fact that any such path \(\gg\) divides the complex plane into two connected open sets of \(\gg\) (the bounded one of which we call the inside or interior of \(\gg\)) is one of the first substantial theorems ever proved in topology, the Jordan Curve Theorem, due to Camille Jordan (1838–1922).

This is the Jordan of Jordan normal form fame, but not the one of Gauß–Jordan elimination.
In this book we shall assume the validity of the Jordan Curve Theorem.
