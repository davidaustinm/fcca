Definition 8.1.14.

Let \(f: G \to \C\) and \(z_0 \in G\text{.}\) If there exist \(R>0\) and \(c_0, c_1, c_2, \ldots \in \C\) such that the power series
\begin{equation*} \s c_k (z-z_0)^k \end{equation*}
converges in \(D[z_0, R]\) and agrees with \(f(z)\) in \(D[z_0, R]\text{,}\) then \(f\) is analytic at \(z_0\text{.}\) We call \(f\) analytic in \(G\) if \(f\) is analytic at each point inÂ \(G\text{.}\)
