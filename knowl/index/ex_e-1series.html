Example 7.1.7.

Consider the sequence \((a_n)\) defined by
\begin{equation*} a_n \ := \ 1 + \frac 1 2 + \frac 1 6 + \dots + \frac 1 {n!} \,\text{.} \end{equation*}
This sequence is increasing (by definition) and each \(a_n \le 3\) by [cross-reference to target(s) "ex_edefdaslimit" missing or not unique]. By the Monotone Sequence Property, \((a_n)\) converges, which allows us to define one of the most famous numbers in all of mathematics,
\begin{equation*} e \ := \ 1 + \lim_{ n \to \infty } a_n \,\text{.} \end{equation*}
